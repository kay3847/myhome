ESSENTIAL CYBER TIPS

Strong Password Hygiene

Use unique, complex passwords for each account. Consider a password manager to keep track of them securely.

Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible

Update Everything Regularly

Keep your operating system, apps, and antivirus software updated to patch security vulnerabilities.

Enable automatic updates when available

Phishing Awareness

Be skeptical of unexpected emails, texts, or calls asking for personal information or urgent action.

Verify sender addresses and hover over links before clicking

Secure Your Network

Use WPA3 encryption for your Wi-Fi, change default router passwords, and consider a VPN for public networks.

Disable WPS and UPnP if not needed

Backup Your Data

Maintain regular backups of important files using the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite.

Test restoring from backups periodically

Privacy Protection

Review privacy settings on social media and apps, be cautious about oversharing, and use privacy-focused tools.

Consider using privacy screen filters in public
